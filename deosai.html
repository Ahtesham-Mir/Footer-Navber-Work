DEOSAI PLAINS

Roof of the World

The Divine Plateau

Deosai Plains, known as "Land of Giants," is one of highest plateaus in the world, situated at an average elevation of 4,114 meters above sea level. This vast alpine plateau spans over 3,000 square kilometers of pristine wilderness between Kashmir and Gilgit-Baltistan.

Accessible only during summer months (June to September) when snow melts, Deosai transforms into a paradise of wildflowers, crystal-clear lakes, and abundant wildlife. The plateau is home to Himalayan brown bears, golden marmots, and over 120 species of birds, making it a crucial biodiversity hotspot.

Sheosar Lake, the "Blind Lake," is crown jewel of Deosai, reflecting surrounding peaks in its turquoise waters. The plateau's unique ecosystem and surreal landscapes create an otherworldly experience, with endless horizons that seem to touch the sky, earning it the nickname "Roof of the World."
