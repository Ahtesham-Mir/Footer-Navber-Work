Pakistan's Wildlife

Explore the diverse fauna that calls Pakistan's national parks home

Markhor

Capra falconeri

Pakistan's national animal, known for its spectacular spiral horns that can grow up to 1.5 meters long. Found in the mountainous regions of northern Pakistan.

Habitat: Hindu Kush, Karakoram, Himalayan foothills

Snow Leopard

Panthera uncia

The elusive ghost of the mountains, perfectly adapted to high-altitude life with thick fur and large paws that act as snowshoes.

Habitat: High mountain ranges of northern Pakistan

Himalayan Brown Bear

Ursus arctos isabellinus

A subspecies of brown bear found in the Himalayas, recognized by its sandy-brown fur and impressive size.

Habitat: Deosai National Park, Himalayan alpine meadows

Marco Polo Sheep

Ovis ammon polii

Named after the famous explorer, this wild sheep is known for its massive spiral horns that can reach 140 cm in length.

Habitat: Khunjerab National Park, high-altitude plateaus

Ladakh Urial

Ovis vignei vignei

A wild sheep species with impressive curved horns, adapted to rugged mountain terrain and harsh climates.

Habitat: Northern mountain ranges, rocky slopes

Western Tragopan

Tragopan melanocephalus

A rare pheasant species with striking black and white plumage and vibrant facial skin. Considered one of the most beautiful pheasants in the world.

Habitat: Coniferous forests of Hazara and Azad Kashmir

Himalayan Monal

Lophophorus impejanus

The national bird of Nepal, famous for the male's iridescent metallic plumage that changes color with viewing angle.

Habitat: Alpine meadows and rhododendron forests

White-headed Duck

Oxyura leucocephala

A small diving duck with a distinctive blue bill and stiff tail feathers. Males have a striking white head during breeding season.

Habitat: Wetlands and lakes of Punjab and Sindh

Egyptian Vulture

Neophron percnopterus

A small vulture species with a distinctive yellow face and white plumage. Known for using tools to break eggs.

Habitat: Mountainous regions, open country

Peregrine Falcon

Falco peregrinus

The world's fastest bird, capable of reaching speeds over 320 km/h during hunting dives. Found across Pakistan's diverse landscapes.

Habitat: Cliffs, mountains, urban areas

Mugger Crocodile

Crocodylus palustris

A medium-sized crocodile found in freshwater habitats across Pakistan. Known for its broad snout and adaptability to various environments.

Habitat: Rivers, lakes, marshes of Sindh and Punjab

Indian Cobra

Naja naja

One of the most venomous snakes in South Asia, recognized by its hood and distinctive spectacle pattern on the back of the hood.

Habitat: Forests, agricultural areas, near human settlements

Bengal Monitor

Varanus bengalensis

A large lizard species that can grow up to 175 cm in length. Known for its intelligence and ability to count to six.

Habitat: Forests, grasslands, agricultural areas

Indian Star Tortoise

Geochelone elegans

A small tortoise species with a distinctive star pattern on its shell. Popular in the pet trade, leading to conservation concerns.

Habitat: Dry areas, scrub forests

Indian Chameleon

Chamaeleo zeylanicus

The only chameleon species found in Pakistan, famous for its ability to change color and independently moving eyes.

Habitat: Trees and bushes in forested areas

Indus River Dolphin

Platanista gangetica minor

A blind freshwater dolphin found only in the Indus River. One of the world's rarest mammals, with fewer than 2,000 individuals remaining.

Habitat: Indus River system in Punjab and Sindh

Golden Mahseer

Tor putitora

A large freshwater fish prized by anglers. Can grow up to 2.75 meters and weigh over 50 kg. Known for its golden coloration.

Habitat: Rivers of northern Pakistan and Kashmir

Giant Catfish

Wallago attu

One of the largest freshwater fish in South Asia, can reach lengths of over 2 meters. An important food fish in local communities.

Habitat: Rivers and lakes across Pakistan

Rainbow Trout

Oncorhynchus mykiss

Introduced to Pakistan's northern rivers for sport fishing. Known for its colorful appearance and fighting ability when caught.

Green Sea Turtle

Chelonia mydas

A large sea turtle found in Pakistan's coastal waters. Important for marine ecosystem health and faces threats from pollution and habitat loss.

Habitat: Coastal waters of Arabian Sea, beaches of Sindh and Balochistan
