OUR STORY

Established in 2024, the National Park of Pakistan was born from a vision to protect the country's most fragile and breathtaking ecosystems. Stretching from the towering peaks of the Karakoram to the rolling deserts of Sindh, our park preserves a living mosaic of wildlife, culture, and history.

For generations, the Markhor — Pakistan's national animal — has stood as a symbol of strength and grace upon these cliffs. Today, alongside snow leopards, brown bears, and hundreds of migratory birds, the Markhor finds protection within our boundaries.

We believe in conservation through community. Local guides, indigenous knowledge keepers, and researchers walk hand in hand to ensure that Pakistan's wild heart beats strong for the generations yet to come.
